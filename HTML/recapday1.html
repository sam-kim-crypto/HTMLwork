Kenya's Economy

Welcome to the Economic Overview of Kenya

Overview

Kenya has one of the largest economies in East Africa. It is a lower-middle-income country with a diverse economy including agriculture, manufacturing, services, and tourism.

Main Economic Sectors

Agriculture remains the backbone of the Kenyan economy, employing over 70% of the rural population. Major experts include tea, coffee, and horticultural products.

The service sector, particularly telecommunications and finance, is rapidly growing and contributes significantly to the country's GDP.

Challenges

Kenya faces challenges such as unemployment, corruption, and infrastructure deficits that affect its economic growth.
